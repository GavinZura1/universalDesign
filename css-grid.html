About the New Orleans Pelicans

The New Orleans Pelicans are a professional basketball team based in New Orleans, Louisiana. Competing in the NBA's Western Conference, the team was established in 2002 and has since become a key part of the cityâ€™s sports culture. Known for their resilience and vibrant fan base, the Pelicans continue to make strides in the league with an exciting roster and dynamic gameplay.

History

Originally known as the New Orleans Hornets, the team changed its name to the Pelicans in 2013, inspired by Louisiana's state bird. Over the years, the Pelicans have had several standout players, including Chris Paul and Anthony Davis. With a dedicated fan following, they bring energy to every game at the Smoothie King Center.

Current Roster

The Pelicans roster is filled with young talent and experienced veterans. Key players include Zion Williamson, Brandon Ingram, and CJ McCollum. Together, they aim to lead the team to new heights and make a significant impact in the playoffs.

Community Involvement

The Pelicans are deeply involved in the New Orleans community, participating in various outreach programs that support youth development, education, and wellness. Through initiatives like the Pelicans Community Assist Program, the team is committed to giving back and building a brighter future for the city.
